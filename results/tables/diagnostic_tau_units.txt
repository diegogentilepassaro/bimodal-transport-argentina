READING - two separate conclusions, do not conflate them: 
 
(1) THE UNIT CONVERSION IS EXACT BY CONSTRUCTION. 03b builds 
conductance = 1/mean(cost) and geoCorrection divides by inter-cell 
distance in metres, so costDistance accumulates (pesos/ton-km) x m. 
Therefore tau_pesos_per_ton = tau_raster_units / 1000, regardless of 
which modes or cells the route crosses. This is the number the 
iceberg normalization (1 + cost/V, V in 1960 pesos/ton) needs; 
tau_units_to_pesos = 1000 is now in config.R section 7b. 
 
(2) THE CORRIDOR RATIOS DIAGNOSE ROUTE PRICING, NOT UNITS. Ratios 
of cached tau to a pure-road geodesic benchmark span 861-3189: 
below 1,000 on the Parana corridor (navigation at 0.621 substitutes 
road at 1.777), and 2-3x above 1,000 on the 1960 inland corridors. 
The inland premium is too large for route deviation alone; it is the 
sparse 1960 network itself - where no road/rail exists, the LCP 
crosses off-network land at cost_land x HMI (~146 pesos/ton-km), 
which dominates those segments. This is the same capilarity 
mechanism the connector experiment (PR #69) quantified, seen from 
the corridor side, and it is substantive (1960 inland transport WAS 
expensive), not a unit artifact. In 1960 pesos per ton the corridors 
read: BA-Rosario ~407, Cordoba-Mendoza ~1,906, Salta-Cordoba ~4,157.
